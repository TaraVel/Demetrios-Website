Education

  • 1971

    Athens College

    Athens College was established in 1925 to bring the best of both Greek and American educational systems to Greece and is considered top in the country. This laid the foundations of my academic career.

  • 1975

    Indiana University of Pennsylvania

    During my time here I studied Biology (BSc Pre-Medical with a minor in History), which trained me well enough to gain admission to Medical School and honored me with the 2001 Distinguished Alumni Award. I have the fondest of memories of my four years of college education in Pennsylvania.

  • 1979

    Northwestern University's Feinberg School of Medicine

    The City of Chicago, Illinois and one of its premier Medical Schools, Northwestern University's Feinberg School of Medicine, hosted me for the next four years. Back then as it still is, the study of medicine was

    no bed of roses. Perseverance helped, in the face of adversity when Public Law 94/484, The health Manpower Act of 1976, prevented me from remaining in the US for a long enough period after my graduation to train for board certification in Neurology. My letter to the editor of the New England Journal of Medicine explains it all. Nevertheless, I am grateful to my teachers at Northwestern Medicine, especially my adviser, the late Prof. Rainer Spehlmann, MD, PhD, who made it possible for me to qualify for admission to the Netherlands Institute for Neuroscience as a research student (1979-1980) after leaving the US with a substantial educational debt and a medical degree in my hand which was yet to be recognized as such outside the USA.

After passing oral and written medical exams in Dutch, a language I learned well enough to succeed thanks to my wife's well-meant and ultimately very appropriate refusal to speak with me in anything but, I obtained medical certification in The Netherlands by virtue of qualifying for a medical degree from the Faculty of Medicine of the University of Amsterdam. Passing another set of oral and written exams, allowed me to obtain certification as a foreign-trained medical doctor in my native Greece. I am probably one of very few people to have been awarded a medical degree from three different institutions in three countries and in as many languages.

  • 1986

    Academisch Medisch Centrum

    Academisch Medisch Centrum (AMC), the teaching hospital of the University of Amsterdam, is where I obtained my post-graduate training in Neurology and Clinical Neurophysiology (1980-1986). Case presentations led by Jan Overweg, MD, PhD, the consultant in epileptology at AMC, kindled my interest in epilepsy.

    My twelve months as a young specialist at the intensive care unit of the Department of Neurosurgery of the same hospital allowed me to appreciate the strengths and limitations of neurosurgery that complement those of neurology. The late Prof. Harry Meinardi, MD, PhD, offered me my first real job and a total immersion course in epilepsy as a neurologist and clinical neurophysiologist at SEIN in Heemstede, The Netherlands for the next 29 years. Thanks to his guidance I became active early on with the International League Against Epilepsy (ILAE) of which he was President at that time. He also made it possible for me to attend the 1989 World Congress of Epilepsy in New Delhi, India, the start of my love affair with that vast country but also of my professional association with Prof. Jerome (Pete) Engel, Jr, MD, PhD, who later allowed me to obtain fellowship training in intracranial EEG and MEG recordings (1990-1991) at the UCLA Geffen School of Medicine's Brain Research Institute in Westwood, Los Angeles, California while on paid leave from SEIN (1990-1991). The ILAE honored me by letting me participate in the various forms of what is currently known as its Diagnostic Methods Commission, during eight years as Commission Member and four years as Neurophysiology sub-commission President. A number of professional recommendations in which I am listed as (co)author, issued from that period and were published in Epilepsia, the official ILAE journal.

Employers

1987 - 2016

Centre of Excellence for Epilepsy and Sleep Medicine afforded me the opportunity to actively engage in patient care and scientific research in epilepsy. SEIN also entrusted the medical guidance of the Department of Clinical Neurophysiology and the Epilepsy Monitoring unit to me as Department Chair (2006-2014). It also allowed me to help set up the SEIN-VU University Medical Center Epilepsy Surgery Program over a period of 15 years.

2016 -

VU University Medical Center, Amsterdam, The Netherlands recognizing my long-standing participation in setting up its epilepsy surgery program, offered me a position as senior epileptologist with the Department of Neurology after my departure from SEIN in 2016.
I am truly honored and delighted to participate in the training my younger colleagues in Amsterdam and guide them along the way to the further advancement of their education in epileptology, to science and, last but not least, the service to our patients who seek our help in a joint effort to render them free of debilitating epileptic seizures.
